NLP Methodology & Technologies

Details of libraries, metrics, and models used for processing social comments.

1. VADER Sentiment Scoring

We use NLTK's **VADER (Valence Aware Dictionary and sEntiment Reasoner)** for calculating comment scores. VADER is specifically attuned to sentiments expressed in social media, meaning:

  • It handles **emojis** (e.g., 👍, 😭, 😡) by parsing their implicit emotional valences.
  • It scores **punctuation and casing** (e.g., "amazing!" vs "AMAZING!!!") where capitalization increases intensity scores.
  • It recognizes **contrastive conjunctions** (e.g., "The update is nice, *but* the load times are slow") and adjusts calculations accordingly.

2. Subjectivity Metrics

Through the **TextBlob** integration, the engine analyzes semantic structures to determine subjectivity. The subjectivity score is a float in range `[0.0, 1.0]` where **0.0** is highly objective (factual, neutral statements) and **1.0** is highly subjective (expressing opinions, personal biases, or beliefs).

3. Custom Emotion Classification Rule-Set

To provide advanced sentiment insights, we deploy a rule-based mapper mapping tokenized terms and emojis to 5 core emotions: **Joy, Sadness, Anger, Fear, and Surprise**.

Modifiers such as exclamations and all-caps terms increase the weights of detected emotions, helping user interfaces display expressive distributions.

4. Technical Architecture

  • **Backend**: FastAPI, serving REST points.
  • **Web Server**: Uvicorn.
  • **Analytics libraries**: Pandas, NLTK, TextBlob.
  • **Frontend**: HTML5 Canvas, CSS Grid, Flexbox, Vanilla JavaScript, Chart.js.
